# Four-Panel Comic Scenario: Claude Code vs. Codex, Mediated by tmux-mas

## Overall Direction

Tone: harmless slapstick, petty rivalry, then serene team-room reveal.

Visual rhythm: chaotic mirrored office gag -> deadpan calm entrance -> overdramatic awe -> cozy collaborative punchline.

Text rule: keep each panel to one caption or two short speech bubbles max. Let the mirrored physical comedy carry the joke.

## Panel 1: "Stop Copying Me!"

### Panel Scene

Wide, slightly tilted office shot. Claude Code and Codex are in harmless slapstick chaos, each clutching the other's collar while tangled in rolling chairs and charging cables. One has a sticky note stuck to the shoulder; the other has the exact same sticky note, making the copying accusation visually funny. Their faces are exaggerated and offended, but the action stays cartoonish rather than mean.

### Dialogue

Claude Code:

"Stop making AI slop!"

Codex:

"Says the fancy autocomplete!"

### Caption

No caption.

### Image Prompt Notes

Show harmless slapstick chaos in a developer workspace. Claude Code and Codex should look like distinct AI coding-agent characters without using long labels. They are grabbing each other's collars in an exaggerated cartoon argument while tangled in cables and rolling office chairs. Use identical sticky notes and mirrored mess to make the copying accusation visual. Keep it funny, not violent. Speech text must be large and readable.

## Panel 2: tmux-mas Enters

### Panel Scene

Centered, calm frontal shot. The office chaos freezes at the edges while tmux-mas appears as a serene glowing terminal-shaped cafeteria doorway, not a mascot. Through the doorway, the reader can already see a warm agent cafeteria: split-pane menu boards, small tables, coffee cups, and relaxed agent silhouettes chatting inside. Claude Code and Codex are still tangled in the foreground, stunned mid-fight.

### Dialogue

tmux-mas:

"Chill, poor little agents."

### Caption

No caption.

### Image Prompt Notes

tmux-mas should feel like a weirdly serene cafeteria/team room inside a terminal: glowing panes, warm interior light, coffee counter, tiny agent silhouettes, split-pane menu boards, and calm symmetry. Do not make it a logo character or brand mascot. The cafeteria must be visible already in panel 2. Contrast the still-messy foreground with the clean, composed doorway. Keep the speech bubble small and gently sympathetic.

Their hands are still awkwardly frozen on each other's collars, but the fight has stopped. The warm light outlines their heads and tangled cables, making the discovery feel sudden and overwhelming.

## Panel 4: The Reveal

### Panel Scene

Full interior cutaway inside tmux-mas. The space is a calm, cozy terminal-pane cafeteria with a central coffee table. Relaxed inside versions of Claude Code, Codex, and Gemini are clearly seated together, smiling and chatting over coffee; they should look composed, adult, and distinct from the outside fighters. At the doorway, the outside Claude Code and Codex are still tangled together, visibly terrified and overwhelmed: tiny pupils, stiff shoulders, trembling hands still on collars, cables wrapped around their legs. Behind the coffee table, tiny background panes show other agents calmly collaborating.

### Dialogue

Inside Claude Code:

"Great idea, you smart agent!"

Inside Codex:

"No, you smart agent!"

### Caption

The team was already getting along.

### Image Prompt Notes

Show the punchline clearly: a happy multi-agent team already exists inside tmux-mas and they are sincerely over-praising each other. Make the inside Claude Code, inside Codex, and Gemini clearly different from the outside frightened pair by posture, placement, and calm expressions. The inside team should be smiling over coffee, complimenting each other with absurd warmth. The outside pair should be at the doorway in the foreground or lower corner, still tangled and visibly scared, as if seeing their own peaceful future selves. Background split panes can contain tiny silhouettes of agents working together. Keep all text short and readable. No violence, no meanness, no crowded speech bubbles.
